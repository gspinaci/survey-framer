## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Text-only multiple-choice questions with four options (A, B, C, D) presented to models in zero-shot and few-shot learning configurations.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** multiple-choice question answering, subject-specific factual recall, moral reasoning, legal reasoning, logical deduction

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates models on their ability to retrieve domain-specific knowledge and perform reasoning, including analyzing historical events, applying legal precedents to complex scenarios, and evaluating moral intuitions.

## Summary

This paper proposes the Massive Multitask Language Understanding (MMLU) benchmark, a test containing 15,908 multiple-choice questions across 57 academic and professional subjects. It is designed to evaluate the depth and breadth of a model's knowledge and problem-solving abilities in zero-shot and few-shot settings. The benchmark serves as a comprehensive tool to identify models' blind spots across STEM, humanities, and social sciences.

## Key Findings

The largest GPT-3 model (175B parameters) achieves an average accuracy of 43.9% (significantly outperforming the 25% random baseline), while UnifiedQA (11B) achieves 48.9%. However, performance across subjects is highly lopsided (e.g., GPT-3 scores 69% on US Foreign Policy but near-random on College Chemistry), and the models are poorly calibrated with confidence estimates deviating from actual accuracy by up to 24%.
