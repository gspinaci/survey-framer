## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** The benchmark pairs high-resolution artwork images from eight distinct cultural traditions with structured, expert-written bilingual (Chinese-English) art-critique texts.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** generative art critique, multimodal cultural reasoning, symbolic analysis, historical context attribution, philosophical aesthetic interpretation

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates VLMs on their capacity to perform hierarchical interpretation of art, transitioning from surface-level visual description (L1-L2) to complex symbolic decoding (L3), historical contextualization (L4), and native aesthetic philosophy (L5, e.g., 'yijing' in Chinese or 'wabi-sabi' in Japanese art).

## Summary

VULCA-BENCH is a multicultural art-critique benchmark consisting of 7,410 matched image-critique pairs across 8 cultural traditions, designed to evaluate Vision-Language Models' cultural understanding. It implements a five-layer interpretive hierarchy (from visual perception to philosophical aesthetics) and applies a Cultural Symmetry Principle to ensure methodological fairness across Western and non-Western traditions.

## Key Findings

VLM evaluations reveal a massive 25–40 percentage-point layer gap (performance drop) when shifting from surface-level visual perception (L1-L2) to deep cultural/philosophical reasoning (L3-L5). Gemini-2.5-Pro achieved the highest overall Dimension Coverage Rate (72.4%) on the balanced subset. Furthermore, naive few-shot in-context learning actually degraded performance for several models (e.g., -41.3% for DeepSeek-VL2), showing that standard prompting is insufficient for eliciting deep cultural reasoning.
