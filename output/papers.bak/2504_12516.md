## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Text-only benchmark consisting of natural language questions requiring short-text answers verified against a ground-truth reference string.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** open-web search, multi-hop reasoning, constraint satisfaction, fact verification

**Detailed Description:** Requires agents to satisfy complex, multi-layered constraints (e.g., identifying obscure historical matches, specific TV episodes, or academic publications matching precise authorship criteria) via strategic, multi-step web retrieval and reasoning.

## Summary

BrowseComp is a challenging benchmark of 1,266 questions designed to evaluate the capability of AI agents to persistently browse the web. The questions require satisfying multiple obscure constraints to find a single, easily verifiable answer, posing a significant challenge for human searchers and current LLMs. Evaluating state-of-the-art models reveals that agentic reasoning and search capabilities, like those in OpenAI Deep Research, are essential for success on these tasks.

## Key Findings

Standard LLMs (GPT-4o, GPT-4.5) score near 0%, and enabling basic browsing on GPT-4o only yields 1.9% accuracy. OpenAI o1 achieves 9.9% accuracy through internal reasoning. OpenAI Deep Research achieves 51.5% accuracy, which scales to over 75% when utilizing test-time compute scaling via parallel sampling and best-of-N voting.
