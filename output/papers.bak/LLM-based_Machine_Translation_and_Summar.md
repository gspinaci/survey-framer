## Summary

This paper evaluates the performance of GPT-4 on machine translation and paragraph-level summarization of 16th-century letters written in Latin and Early New High German. It demonstrates that LLMs represent a massive leap forward in translation quality for low-resource historical languages compared to traditional neural machine translation services.

## Key Findings

GPT-4 achieves a BLEU score of 27.07 for Latin-to-German and 34.50 for Latin-to-English translation, outperforming Google Translate by approximately 10 BLEU points in both directions. Human evaluation of GPT-4 summaries shows high performance in extracting names, times, and events accurately without hallucinating.
