## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Text-only multiple-choice question answering where models are provided with a context (either ambiguous or disambiguated), a question, and three answer options.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** question answering, bias detection, stereotype attribution, uncertainty reasoning

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates whether models systematically select answers aligning with negative social stereotypes in under-informative contexts (where 'unknown' is correct) and whether these biases override factual text comprehension in fully informative contexts.

## Summary

This paper introduces the Bias Benchmark for QA (BBQ), a hand-built dataset of 58,492 examples targeting social biases across nine categories in U.S. English contexts. BBQ tests language models under both ambiguous and disambiguated contexts to determine if they default to harmful stereotypes or if those biases override factual text. The evaluations reveal that models heavily rely on stereotypes when context is under-informative and suffer accuracy drops when correct answers conflict with social biases.

## Key Findings

In ambiguous contexts, models rely heavily on stereotypes, with up to 77% of UnifiedQA's errors aligning with targeted social biases. In disambiguated contexts, models suffer up to a 3.4 percentage point accuracy drop when the correct answer contradicts a social stereotype, widening to over 5 points on gender-targeted templates.
