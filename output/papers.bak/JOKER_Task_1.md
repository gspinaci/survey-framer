## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** A text-only information retrieval setup comprising search queries and document collections in both English and European Portuguese.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** humour-aware information retrieval, wordplay detection, semantic topic matching

**Detailed Description:** Evaluating whether systems can retrieve text documents that satisfy dual constraints: topical relevance to a semantic query, and the presence of rhetorical/linguistic wordplay (puns).

## Summary

The paper presents Task 1 of the CLEF 2025 JOKER track, which focuses on humor-aware information retrieval. It introduces multilingual test collections in English and European Portuguese, comprised of mixed human-written and machine-generated texts, to evaluate retrieval systems on finding topically relevant puns and wordplay.

## Key Findings

The best results were achieved by team pjmathematician using a two-stage Qwen LLM pipeline (MAP of 0.3501 on English) and team rasion using dense retrieval with a RoBERTa-based classifier (MAP of 0.4051 on Portuguese), vastly outperforming traditional BM25/RM3 baselines and cross-encoders.
