## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Monomodal text-based multiple-choice question answering (MCQs) across 11 Indic languages and English.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** multiple-choice question answering, cultural knowledge retrieval, historical reasoning, legal and governance comprehension, linguistic adaptation

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates factual knowledge retrieval and cultural/historical reasoning on region-specific topics (e.g., local administrative laws, regional historical events, and traditional Indian performing arts) sourced from qualification and civil service exams.

## Summary

MILU (Multi-task Indic Language Understanding) is a comprehensive multi-task benchmark designed to evaluate LLMs across 11 Indic languages and English. It consists of approximately 79K multiple-choice questions spanning 8 domains and 41 subjects curated from Indian civil service and regional exams, placing a strong emphasis on culturally specific and localized knowledge.

## Key Findings

GPT-4o achieved the highest overall average accuracy at 74.74%. Current LLMs generally struggle with MILU; specialized language-specific fine-tuned models often perform near random baselines, while open multilingual models (like Llama-3.1 and Gemma-2) perform better. Models consistently perform worse in low-resource and agglutinative languages (such as Tamil and Telugu) and show weaker performance in culturally grounded domains like Arts & Humanities compared to general STEM subjects.
