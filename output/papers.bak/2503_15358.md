## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Integrates natural language sentences containing potentially idiomatic nominal compounds with generated static images (Subtask A) or sequential image panels representing visual narratives (Subtask B). It also offers a text-only modality using descriptive image captions.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** multimodal image ranking, next image prediction, sentence classification

**Detailed Description:** Tasks require identifying whether a nominal compound is used in a literal or figurative context, ranking generated illustrations based on semantic alignment, and predicting sequential visual outcomes of idiomatic scenarios.

## Summary

This paper introduces AdMIRe (SemEval-2025 Task 1), a benchmark designed to evaluate how well vision-language models represent idiomatic expressions in English and Brazilian Portuguese. The benchmark features two tasks: ranking images based on their alignment with idiomatic or literal context sentences, and predicting the next image in a narrative sequence. The top-performing submissions used mixture-of-experts settings with large language and vision models to achieve human-level performance.

## Key Findings

The top-performing systems achieved up to 93% Top-1 accuracy on the English Subtask A test set and 100% on the Portuguese test set, matching or slightly exceeding human-expert baselines (human average of 71% accuracy, pool-of-experts at 83%). Achieving this performance required complex mixture-of-experts pipelines, data augmentation, and targeted prompt engineering to mitigate generative models' inherent biases towards literal interpretations.
