## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Evaluates text-based prompts and LLM-generated narratives regarding cultural heritage sites, museum artifacts, and historical events.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** value alignment assessment, cultural sensitivity detection, historical bias identification, factual representation analysis

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates whether LLM-generated cultural heritage texts align with localized ethical values, historical facts, and cultural sensitivities, identifying cases of Western/Eurocentric bias or misrepresentation of marginalized heritage.

## Summary

This paper introduces CHVM-1K, a benchmark dataset specifically designed to investigate and quantify value misalignment in LLM-generated texts for cultural heritage. It evaluates the risks associated with deploying LLMs in museums and heritage sites, focusing on how these models construct narratives around historical artifacts.

## Key Findings

The paper demonstrates that popular LLMs exhibit significant value misalignments, including historical distortion, Western-centric biases, and cultural insensitivities, when generating heritage-related content. Larger models show improved factual consistency but still suffer from subtle narrative biases.
