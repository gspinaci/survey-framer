## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** image, text

**Description:** Evaluates multimodal models using artwork images (paintings) alongside textual prompts featuring either class labels (saint names) or detailed semantic descriptions from the Iconclass classification system.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** image classification, zero-shot learning, few-shot learning

**Detailed Description:** Performs single-label iconographical image classification to identify 10 distinct categories of Christian saints under zero-shot conditions (using names vs. detailed descriptions) and few-shot learning conditions.

## Summary

This study benchmarks the performance of Vision-Language Models (CLIP, SigLIP) and Multimodal Large Language Models (GPT-4o, Gemini 2.5) on classifying Christian iconography. Using curated subsets of three cultural heritage datasets (ArtDL, ICONCLASS, and Wikidata) representing the ten most common saints, the authors test models under zero-shot (labels only), description-enriched, and few-shot configurations. The results reveal that advanced multimodal LLMs can outperform traditional fine-tuned supervised baselines, though performance remains sensitive to data consistency and formatting.

## Key Findings

Gemini 2.5 Pro and GPT-4o achieved high accuracies on ArtDL (90.45% and 88.20% respectively) and ICONCLASS (84.82% and 75.43% respectively), outperforming a fine-tuned ResNet50 baseline. Conversely, the Wikidata dataset proved challenging for LLMs, with the contrastive model SigLIP-SO400M-Patch14-384 reaching the top accuracy of 66.29%. Enriching prompts with Iconclass descriptions generally improved zero-shot performance, whereas few-shot learning with five exemplars typically resulted in lower accuracies.
