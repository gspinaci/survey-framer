## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** vision, text

**Description:** Multi-modal evaluation combining artwork images with generated bilingual (Chinese-English) art critiques that are assessed across five levels of cultural understanding (L1-L5)

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** art critique generation, cultural interpretation, iconographic analysis, aesthetic evaluation, historical contextualization

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates VLMs' ability to generate expert-level art critiques spanning five levels: L1 (visual perception), L2 (technical analysis), L3 (cultural symbolism), L4 (historical context), and L5 (philosophical aesthetics). Tasks require deep cultural understanding including iconographic interpretation, aesthetic theory application, and cross-cultural art analysis

## Summary

This paper introduces VULCA-BENCH, a cross-cultural benchmark for evaluating Vision-Language Models' art critique capabilities using a tri-tier framework that measures cultural understanding across five levels from visual perception to philosophical aesthetics. The benchmark covers 6,695 image-critique pairs across six cultural traditions with 165 culture-specific dimensions, revealing that VLMs show degraded performance from surface-level description to deeper cultural interpretation, with systematic bias favoring Western art.

## Key Findings

Evaluation of 15 VLMs revealed that cultural understanding degrades from L1-L2 (visual description) to L3-L5 (cultural interpretation), with Depth (σ=0.56) and Alignment (σ=0.48) being most discriminative. 13 of 15 models showed bias toward Western art (Cohen's d = -0.74, p < 0.001). Automated metrics and judge scoring measure different constructs (ICC < 0.2), establishing single-judge calibration as more reliable than dual-judge averaging.
