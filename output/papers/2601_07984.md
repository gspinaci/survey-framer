## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** visual, text

**Description:** Multi-modal evaluation combining artwork images with bilingual text generation - VLMs receive artwork images and generate bilingual (Chinese-English) art critiques covering L1-L5 levels of cultural understanding

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** cultural interpretation, art critique generation, symbolic analysis, historical contextualization, aesthetic evaluation

**Detailed Description:** Five-level analytical hierarchy from visual perception (L1-L2) to deep cultural interpretation (L3-L5): L1 visual perception, L2 technical analysis, L3 cultural symbolism, L4 historical context, L5 philosophical aesthetics. Tasks require interpreting culture-specific concepts, identifying artistic techniques, and contextualizing within art-historical traditions

## Summary

VULCA-BENCH introduces a tri-tier evaluation framework for assessing Vision-Language Models' cultural understanding in art interpretation across six cultural traditions. The framework combines automated metrics, single-judge rubric scoring, and human expert calibration to evaluate cultural competence from visual description (L1-L2) to deep cultural interpretation (L3-L5).

## Key Findings

VLMs show degraded performance from visual description (L1-L2) to cultural interpretation (L3-L5), with Critique Depth (σ=0.56) and Cultural Alignment (σ=0.48) being most discriminative. 13 of 15 models exhibit systematic bias favoring Western art over non-Western traditions (Cohen's d = -0.74, p < 0.001). Automated metrics and judge scoring measure different constructs (ICC < 0.2).
