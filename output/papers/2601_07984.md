## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Multi-modal evaluation combining artwork images with bilingual (Chinese and English) textual prompts that require models to generate structured art critiques.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** generative art critique, technical visual analysis, symbolic decoding, historical context attribution, philosophical aesthetics interpretation

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates open-ended critiques across five analytical levels: sensory recognition of forms and colors (L1), technical analysis of artistic medium and brushstrokes (L2), decoding of cultural symbolism (L3), attribution of historical and biographical context (L4), and interpretation of philosophical and aesthetic concepts (L5).

## Summary

The paper introduces VULCA-BENCH, a tri-tier evaluation framework for assessing the cross-cultural art critique capabilities of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) across six global art traditions. It maps artistic understanding onto a theoretical five-level hierarchy (from basic visual description to deep philosophical interpretation) and applies sigmoid calibration to align LLM-judge scores with human expert evaluations. Testing on 15 state-of-the-art VLMs reveals systematic performance degradation at deeper interpretive levels and a significant, persistent bias favoring Western art.

## Key Findings

Gemini-2.5-Pro (4.27) and Qwen3-VL-235B (4.21) achieved the highest calibrated scores, while models generally degraded in performance when moving from surface visual description (L1-L2) to cultural interpretation (L3-L5). Critically, 13 of the 15 evaluated models scored Western art significantly higher than non-Western art (overall China-West gap Cohen's d = -0.74, p < 0.001).
