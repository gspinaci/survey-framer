## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** vision, text

**Description:** Multi-modal evaluation combining artwork images with bilingual (Chinese-English) text generation for art critique, using a tri-tier framework with automated metrics, judge scoring, and human calibration

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** cultural interpretation, art critique generation, iconographic analysis, aesthetic evaluation, historical contextualization

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates five levels of cultural understanding from visual perception (L1) through technical analysis (L2) to cultural symbolism (L3), historical context (L4), and philosophical aesthetics (L5). Tasks require iconographic interpretation, culture-specific terminology usage, and deep aesthetic understanding rather than factual recognition

## Summary

This paper introduces VULCA-BENCH, a cross-cultural vision-language benchmark for evaluating VLM cultural understanding in art critique across six traditions. The tri-tier evaluation framework combines automated metrics, single-judge scoring, and human calibration to assess five levels of cultural understanding from visual perception to philosophical aesthetics.

## Key Findings

Cultural understanding degrades from visual description (L1-L2) to cultural interpretation (L3-L5), with Depth (σ=0.56) and Alignment (σ=0.48) as most discriminative dimensions. 13 of 15 VLMs show systematic bias favoring Western art (Cohen's d = -0.74, p < 0.001). Automated metrics and judge scoring measure different constructs (ICC < 0.2).
