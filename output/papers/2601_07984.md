## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Combines visual artwork images with bilingual (Chinese-English) natural language prompts and expert critiques to evaluate generative cultural art interpretation.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** generative art critique, visual description, technical art analysis, cultural symbolism decoding, art-historical attribution, philosophical aesthetic interpretation

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates models across a five-level hierarchy of interpretation derived from Panofsky's iconology: visual perception (L1), technical analysis (L2), cultural symbolism (L3), historical context and attribution (L4), and philosophical aesthetics (L5).

## Summary

The paper presents VULCA-BENCH (via the VULCA-Framework), a tri-tier evaluation framework designed to benchmark the cross-cultural competence of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) on expert-level art critique generation. Grounded in Panofsky's iconological levels and Goodman's theories, it measures five distinct layers of interpretive depth, from basic visual description to deep philosophical aesthetics, while addressing systemic evaluation disparities by calibrating single-judge LLM scores against human expert annotations.

## Key Findings

The evaluation of 15 VLMs reveals that while models excel at surface-level visual description (L1-L2), cultural competence degrades significantly on deeper interpretive tasks (L3-L5) requiring cultural alignment and historical context. Additionally, 13 out of 15 models consistently demonstrate a persistent bias favoring Western art over non-Western traditions (Cohen's d = -0.74, p < 0.001). The study also finds standard automated evaluation and dual-judge averaging highly unreliable (ICC < 0.2), validating the paper's single-judge sigmoid calibration to align automated outputs with human experts.
