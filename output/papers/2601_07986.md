## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Multimodal dataset pairing high-resolution artwork images from diverse cultures with expert-written bilingual (Chinese and English) art critiques.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** generative art critique, visual perception analysis, technical art analysis, cultural symbolism decoding, historical context attribution, philosophical aesthetics reasoning

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates generative cultural understanding using a five-layer ascending framework: L1 (visual perception), L2 (technical analysis), L3 (cultural symbolism), L4 (historical context), and L5 (philosophical aesthetics).

## Summary

VULCA-BENCH is a multicultural art-critique benchmark containing 7,410 image-critique pairs across eight traditions, designed to evaluate the depth of cultural understanding in Vision-Language Models. By adopting a five-layer hierarchical framework (from basic visual perception to philosophical aesthetics) and enforcing a Cultural Symmetry Principle, it provides a rigorous basis for evaluating AI systems on complex, domain-expert analytical reasoning.

## Key Findings

State-of-the-art VLMs suffer a dramatic 25-40 percentage-point performance drop when transitioning from visual/technical perception (L1-L2) to cultural/philosophical interpretation (L3-L5). Error analyses indicate models rely on superficial terminology, exhibit historical anachronisms, and conflate cultural traditions. Additionally, naive few-shot prompting degrades performance for most models.
