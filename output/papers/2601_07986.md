## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Vision-language evaluation combining artwork images with bilingual (Chinese-English) expert critiques. VLMs generate cultural art critiques that are evaluated against expert-written critiques across 225 culture-specific dimensions

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** cultural art critique generation, hierarchical cultural understanding, cross-cultural interpretation, symbolic reasoning, philosophical aesthetic analysis

**Detailed Description:** Five-layer framework (L1-L5) progressing from visual perception and technical analysis to cultural symbolism, historical context, and philosophical aesthetics. Tasks require deep cultural knowledge including understanding concepts like Chinese qiyun (spirit resonance), Japanese wabi-sabi, Islamic geometric principles, and Western iconographic traditions

## Summary

VULCA-BENCH introduces a comprehensive multicultural art critique benchmark with 7,410 image-critique pairs across 8 cultural traditions, operationalized through a five-layer framework (L1-L5) progressing from visual perception to philosophical aesthetics. The benchmark includes 225 culture-specific dimensions and bilingual expert critiques to evaluate VLMs' hierarchical cultural understanding beyond surface-level visual perception.

## Key Findings

All evaluated VLMs show a consistent 25-40 percentage-point performance drop from visual perception tasks (L1-L2) to cultural interpretation tasks (L3-L5), with Dimension Coverage Rates ranging from 58.2% to 72.4%. Error analysis reveals systematic failures including surface-level terminology use, historical anachronism, and cultural conflation, demonstrating that cultural understanding represents a qualitatively different capability tier from visual perception.
