## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** The benchmark presents models with image inputs of heritage objects and natural language prompts, requiring them to output structured text metadata (JSON format) containing predicted cultural attributes.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** structured metadata inference, cultural reasoning, temporal attribution, provenance reasoning, creator attribution

**Detailed Description:** Models must perform contextual and cultural reasoning to infer non-observable historical attributes of artworks (culture, production period, geographic origin, and artist/creator attribution) from visual input.

## Summary

The paper introduces Appear2Meaning, a cross-cultural benchmark designed to evaluate how well vision-language models can infer structured, non-observable cultural metadata (culture, period, origin, creator) from images of heritage artifacts. Using 750 validated museum objects, the study formalizes heritage understanding as a structured prediction task and utilizes an LLM-as-a-Judge framework. Findings demonstrate that while models capture partial stylistic signals, they struggle with coherent multi-attribute metadata inference and exhibit systematic cross-cultural biases.

## Key Findings

All 9 evaluated VLMs achieved very low exact-match accuracy (0.005 to 0.045), demonstrating the difficulty of joint multi-attribute prediction. However, partial match rates were significantly higher, with Qwen3-VL-Flash leading at 0.658. Performance varied strongly by region, with the highest accuracy in East Asia and the lowest in Europe and the Americas, where models often relied on stylistic shortcuts and Eurocentric misattributions.
