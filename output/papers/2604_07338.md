## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** image, text

**Description:** Multi-modal evaluation where models receive image-only inputs of cultural heritage objects and generate structured text predictions (JSON metadata) evaluated against textual ground truth.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** structured metadata inference, cultural reasoning, attribute prediction

**Detailed Description:** Evaluating the inference of non-observable historical and contextual attributes—such as cultural classification, historical period, geographic origin, and creator attribution—from visual evidence.

## Summary

The paper introduces Appear2Meaning, a cross-cultural benchmark designed to evaluate vision-language models on structured cultural metadata inference from image-only inputs. Using a dataset of 750 validated museum artifacts spanning four cultural regions and four object types, the benchmark measures the models' capacity to infer non-observable attributes like creator, origin, and period using an LLM-as-a-Judge framework.

## Key Findings

All evaluated VLMs perform poorly on exact match accuracy (ranging from 0.005 to 0.029), but show significantly higher partial match rates (up to 0.658 for Qwen3-VL-Flash). Models demonstrate substantial cultural variation, achieving stronger performance in East Asian contexts than European or American ones, and frequently default to Eurocentric stylistic shortcuts and period compression.
