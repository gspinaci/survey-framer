## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Text-only QA formatted as multiple-choice questions (CulturalBench-Easy) and binary True/False judgments for all options (CulturalBench-Hard).

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** factual recognition, multiple-choice question answering, binary classification

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates factual recognition of regional customs, social etiquette, and language markers; assesses models' ability to recognize scenarios with multiple valid cultural answers (multi-mode behavior) rather than over-converging on a single mode.

## Summary

This paper introduces CulturalBench, a text-based benchmark of 1,696 questions spanning 45 global regions and 17 cultural topics to evaluate the multicultural knowledge of LLMs. Developed through a human-AI collaborative red-teaming pipeline, the dataset includes multiple-choice (Easy) and multi-label binary True/False (Hard) evaluation formats to test model understanding of both single-answer and multi-answer cultural nuances.

## Key Findings

While frontier LLMs achieve near-human performance on the Easy multiple-choice version (up to 89.6%), they struggle significantly on the Hard binary format, with the best model (OpenAI o1) reaching only 61.5% compared to humans' 92.4%. Models exhibit substantial mode-seeking biases, failing heavily on multi-mode questions (average 20.9% accuracy compared to 49.6% for single-mode), and systematically underperform on questions related to less-represented regions like South America, Eastern Europe, and the Middle East.
