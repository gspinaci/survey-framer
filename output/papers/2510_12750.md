## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Multi-modal VQA combining artwork images with natural language questions that require deep visual understanding of artistic content, symbolism, and cultural context

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** Instance Identity, Instance Attribute, Instance Location, Instance Counting, Spatial Relation, Instance Interaction, Visual-Inspired Reasoning

**Detailed Description:** Seven reasoning dimensions from basic perception (identifying objects, attributes, locations, counting) to complex inference (spatial relationships, interactions, and visual-inspired reasoning about symbolic meaning, narratives, and cultural context in artworks)

## Summary

VQArt-Bench introduces a new large-scale VQA benchmark for cultural heritage that uses a novel multi-agent pipeline to generate semantically rich, contextually grounded questions about artworks. The benchmark addresses limitations of rule-based approaches by creating linguistically diverse questions that test deep visual understanding across seven reasoning dimensions.

## Key Findings

Evaluation of 14 state-of-the-art MLLMs revealed significant limitations, with most models failing to achieve 50% accuracy. Models showed surprising weakness in simple counting tasks while excelling in visual-inspired reasoning. Closed-source models (Gemini 2.5 at 71% accuracy) significantly outperformed open-source models, with Kimi-VL being the best open-source model at 67% accuracy.
