## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Multi-modal VQA combining artwork images with natural language questions in multiple-choice format, requiring joint visual and linguistic understanding for art analysis

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** Instance Identity, Instance Attribute, Instance Location, Instance Counting, Spatial Relation, Instance Interaction, Visual-Inspired Reasoning

**Detailed Description:** Seven-dimensional evaluation framework ranging from basic visual perception (identifying objects, attributes, locations, counting) to complex artistic interpretation (spatial relationships, symbolic interactions, and causal/contextual reasoning about artworks)

## Summary

VQArt-Bench introduces a large-scale VQA benchmark for art and cultural heritage, featuring 14,463 semantically rich questions generated through a novel multi-agent pipeline. The benchmark addresses limitations of rule-based VQA approaches by creating linguistically diverse, contextually grounded questions that test deep visual understanding of artworks across seven reasoning dimensions.

## Key Findings

Evaluation of 14 state-of-the-art MLLMs revealed significant performance limitations, with most models failing to reach 50% accuracy. Models unexpectedly struggled with instance counting tasks while excelling in visual-inspired reasoning. Closed-source models like Gemini 2.5 (71% accuracy) outperformed open-source alternatives, though Kimi-VL emerged as the best open-source model at 67% accuracy.
