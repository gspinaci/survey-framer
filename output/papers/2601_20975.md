## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Text-based agentic workflows evaluating prompts by querying, retrieving, and synthesizing text from the open web.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** structured retrieval, context management, logical reasoning, entity resolution, stopping criteria reasoning

**Detailed Description:** Tests the agent's ability to construct multi-step search strategies, manage massive document contexts, resolve naming variations (entity resolution), apply logical constraint verification, and determine stopping criteria under epistemic uncertainty.

## Summary

DeepSearchQA is a 900-prompt benchmark designed to evaluate autonomous web agents on complex, multi-step information retrieval tasks requiring exhaustive answer-set generation. It focuses on the 'Comprehensiveness Gap' by assessing an agent's ability to systematically aggregate fragmented data, resolve overlapping entities, and determine appropriate stopping criteria under epistemic uncertainty. The evaluation employs strict set-based metrics to heavily penalize both under-retrieval and hedging.

## Key Findings

Gemini Deep Research Agent and GPT-5 Pro High Reasoning represent the state-of-the-art, achieving F1 scores of 81.90% and 78.98% respectively, though exhibiting a 'Last Mile Problem' characterized by under- or over-retrieval that reduces their fully correct rates. A steep performance cliff exists for smaller models (e.g., Gemini 2.5 Flash drops to a 42.99% F1 score), illustrating a hard reasoning threshold. Increasing test-time compute via multiple samples monotonically scales accuracy.
