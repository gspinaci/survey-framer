## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Text-only benchmark evaluating models on native-language prompts in French, Spanish, and Chinese, along with a subset of human-translated English equivalents for cross-lingual comparison.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** language-specific linguistic reasoning, wordplay & riddles, cultural/tradition reasoning, math reasoning with cultural relevance

**Detailed Description:** Operationalizes multi-step reasoning over language-specific grammatical conventions, decoding homophonic puns and ancient idioms, performing temporal calculations using traditional historical or religious calendars, and resolving math word problems anchored in regional socio-economic history and measurements.

## Summary

MultiNRC is a native, multilingual reasoning benchmark comprising 1,055 questions in French, Spanish, and Chinese designed to evaluate LLMs on culturally and linguistically nuanced reasoning. Authored by native speakers, it avoids English-centric translation biases by testing deep linguistic, idiomatic, and cultural-historical knowledge. The study reveals that state-of-the-art models struggle significantly with native reasoning, exposing systemic deficiencies in their localized cultural knowledge.

## Key Findings

No evaluated LLM surpasses 50% accuracy on MultiNRC, with the highest-performing model (o3-pro) reaching only 49%. Translating culturally relevant math questions to English improves accuracy by an average of 10-19%, indicating better retrieval of regional knowledge in English. However, scores on deep cultural and traditional reasoning remain stagnant even when translated, demonstrating that models fundamentally lack this localized cultural-historical knowledge regardless of the prompt language.
