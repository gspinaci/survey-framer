## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Text-only reading comprehension benchmark consisting of written trivia questions and corresponding web/Wikipedia text documents.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** reading comprehension, open-domain question answering, multi-sentence reasoning

**Detailed Description:** Requires resolving complex question semantics, handling syntactic and lexical variations, and performing reasoning across multiple sentences in long documents to extract correct answer spans.

## Summary

The paper introduces TriviaQA, a large-scale reading comprehension dataset of over 650K question-answer-evidence triples. TriviaQA uses organically authored questions from trivia enthusiasts coupled with retrospectively collected Web and Wikipedia documents, creating a realistic and challenging benchmark.

## Key Findings

The best-performing baseline, a modified BiDAF model, achieves ~40% exact match under distant supervision on both Wikipedia and Web domains. This performance lags significantly behind the human baseline of approximately 80%, showing that the benchmark is highly challenging due to lexical variation and multi-sentence reasoning demands.
