## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, images, visual-temporal sequences

**Description:** Multi-modal benchmark combining text context sentences with static images and temporal image sequences. Images are generated to represent literal vs idiomatic meanings of expressions, with captions provided for text-only evaluation.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** image ranking, next image prediction, idiomaticity detection, semantic representation

**Detailed Description:** Tasks require ranking images by alignment with idiomatic/literal meanings in context, predicting sequence completions, and distinguishing between literal and figurative interpretations - all requiring deep semantic understanding rather than surface pattern matching

## Summary

This paper presents AdMIRe, a multimodal benchmark for evaluating how well language models understand idiomatic expressions through visual representation tasks. The benchmark includes two subtasks: ranking images by their alignment with literal vs idiomatic meanings, and predicting the next image in sequences depicting idiomatic expressions.

## Key Findings

Top-performing systems achieved human-level performance (93% accuracy) by leveraging mixture-of-experts approaches with multiple LLM queries, though this required substantial computational resources and prompt engineering to overcome models' bias toward literal interpretations.
