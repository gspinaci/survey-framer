## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Combines natural language sentences containing idiomatic expressions with static images (multimodal VQA ranking) and temporal narrative image sequences (visual sequence prediction). Descriptive text captions are also available for text-only settings.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** ranking, classification, sequence prediction, semantic disambiguation

**Detailed Description:** Requires models to perform multimodal image-text ranking of candidate images against a literal/idiomatic target context, binary classification of contextual sentence types, and sequential narrative prediction for three-panel visual storylines.

## Summary

This paper presents SemEval-2025 Task 1: AdMIRe, a benchmark designed to evaluate how well language and vision-language models interpret idiomatic expressions in multimodal and multilingual contexts (English and Portuguese). It features two main subtasks: ranking static images based on their alignment with a target expression's literal or figurative meaning, and predicting the correct final image in a sequential three-panel narrative.

## Key Findings

Top-performing systems approached human-level baseline performance (up to 93% top-1 accuracy in English and 100% in Portuguese for Subtask A). However, achieving this required complex, resource-intensive pipelines such as mixture-of-experts ensembles, chain-of-thought prompt smoothing, and translating idioms into compositional synonyms to bypass the standard VLMs' bias toward interpreting expressions as literal regardless of context.
