## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Evaluates long-form text responses (biographies) generated by language models in response to natural language prompts, analyzing their factual accuracy against Wikipedia text.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** generation, fact verification, information extraction

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates the generation of long-form biographies by breaking them down into 'atomic facts' and verifying each against a reference knowledge source (Wikipedia) to test basic factual recognition and accuracy.

## Summary

FActScore introduces a fine-grained evaluation framework for measuring the factual precision of long-form text generated by LLMs by decomposing texts into atomic facts and verifying them against a reliable corpus. Applying this framework to people biographies, the authors demonstrate the severity of LLM hallucinations and propose a highly accurate automated estimator to replace costly human evaluation. Their extensive comparative study reveals that even state-of-the-art and retrieval-augmented models struggle with factual accuracy, particularly on rare entities.

## Key Findings

ChatGPT achieves only a 58.3% FActScore on biographies, which drops precipitously to 16% as the subject entities become rarer. Retrieval-augmented systems like PerplexityAI improve precision to 71.5% but still struggle with temporal and entity-linking errors. The automated evaluator based on retrieval and model-based checking estimates FActScore with an error rate of less than 2% compared to human annotations.
