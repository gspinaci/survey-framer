## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Evaluates long-form text generated by LLMs against a text-based reference knowledge source (Wikipedia).

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** factual verification, long-form text generation, information extraction

**Detailed Description:** Decomposing generated biographies into short, independent atomic facts and verifying whether each claim is supported by a reliable reference document (Wikipedia) to calculate factual precision.

## Summary

This paper introduces FActScore, an evaluation metric that breaks down long-form LLM-generated texts into individual atomic facts to measure their factual precision against a reliable knowledge source. Since human evaluation is costly, the authors develop an automated model combining passage retrieval and competitive language models to estimate FActScore with less than a 2% error rate. They utilize this automated metric to benchmark 13 recent language models on a dataset of 6,500 biography generations.

## Key Findings

ChatGPT achieves a human-evaluated FActScore of 58.3% on biography generation, which drops to 16% for rare entities. GPT-4 and ChatGPT are significantly more factual than public models, with Vicuna and Alpaca emerging as the best-performing open-source models, although all LMs remain substantially less factual than humans.
