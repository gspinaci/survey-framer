## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** The benchmark consists entirely of text-only multiple-choice questions (MCQs) in 11 Indic languages and English.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** multiple-choice question answering, factual recognition

**Detailed Description:** Models are evaluated primarily on factual recognition and retrieval related to regional history, arts, festivals, grammar, and laws of India via MMLU-style multiple-choice questions. They do not test deep interpretive or causal-reasoning capabilities.

## Summary

This paper introduces MILU (Multi-task Indic Language Understanding), a comprehensive multi-task benchmark spanning 41 subjects across 11 Indic languages and English. Sourced from competitive national and state-level exams, MILU evaluates both general knowledge and India-specific cultural nuances. The authors perform an extensive evaluation of over 42 LLMs, revealing substantial gaps in their multilingual and culturally specific performance.

## Key Findings

GPT-4o achieved the highest average accuracy at 74.74%. Open-source multilingual models significantly outperformed domain-specific Indic models, which often performed near the random baseline. Furthermore, models performed notably worse on culturally relevant domains like Arts & Humanities compared to general STEM subjects, suggesting a critical lack of local cultural data in training corpora.
