## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Visual question answering dataset that pairs natural language questions (English and Italian) with low-to-medium resolution artwork images to evaluate multimodal Visual Question Answering (VQA).

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** factual recognition, visual reasoning, stylistic reasoning, iconographic interpretation, error identification, text completion, word positioning

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates a model's capacity for factual recall of art history, visual and stylistic reasoning (e.g., reading symbolic meaning from visual details), alongside structured text tasks like word positioning, filling in blanks, and identifying historical or factual errors in written texts.

## Summary

EduArt is an educational-level benchmark containing 871 human-authored questions from AP Art History and MyZanichelli Italian textbook exercises to diagnose art-historical knowledge and visual reasoning in multimodal LLMs. The study applies Classical Test Theory to assess item-level difficulty and analyzes how different formats (such as multiple choice, open completion, and error identification) affect model accuracy.

## Key Findings

While six frontier models scored over 90% accuracy on standard multiple-choice questions, performance dropped substantially on more complex formats like open completion (Claude Opus 4.6 fell to 23.9%) and error identification (Claude Sonnet 4.6 fell to 6.2%). Furthermore, requiring models to justify their answers (motivation condition) improved Claude models slightly but degraded performance for GPT and Gemini models, demonstrating that task format variation reveals significant vulnerabilities.
