## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** single-label image classification, zero-shot classification, few-shot classification

**Detailed Description:** Assesses fine-grained visual recognition of Christian saints by matching visual features in artwork to iconographic concepts. Tasks include zero-shot classification (using simple saint names or detailed descriptive texts) and few-shot in-context learning.

## Summary

This study benchmarks Vision-Language Models and Multimodal Large Language Models on single-label image classification of Christian iconography. Using subsets of ArtDL, ICONCLASS, and Wikidata, the authors evaluate models in zero-shot and few-shot settings to assess their potential for metadata curation in digital humanities. The findings show that advanced LLMs can outperform fine-tuned baselines, though performance is highly sensitive to dataset consistency and few-shot exemplar selection.

## Key Findings

Gemini 2.5 Pro and GPT-4o achieved peak accuracies of 90.45% and 88.20% on the ArtDL dataset, respectively, outperforming fine-tuned ResNet50 baselines. However, performance dropped significantly on the Wikidata dataset, where the contrastive model SigLIP-SO400M-Patch14-384 performed best (66.29%). Adding rich iconographic descriptions generally improved zero-shot accuracy, while few-shot learning unexpectedly degraded or only minimally improved performance for most LLMs.
