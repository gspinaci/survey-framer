## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Multi-modal evaluation combining Christian iconographic artwork images with natural language prompts for classification tasks, including class labels, detailed descriptions, and few-shot examples

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** image classification, iconographic recognition, zero-shot classification, few-shot learning

**Detailed Description:** Single-label classification of Christian saints and religious figures in artwork, requiring semantic understanding of iconographic elements, symbols, and religious themes. Tasks evaluate factual recognition of specific saints, contextual interpretation using Iconclass descriptions, and adaptation through few-shot exemplars

## Summary

This study benchmarks Vision-Language Models and Multimodal Large Language Models for classifying Christian iconography in artwork using three datasets (ArtDL, ICONCLASS, Wikidata) across zero-shot and few-shot scenarios. The research evaluates how models perform on single-label classification of Christian saints and religious figures, comparing performance with different prompt enrichment strategies.

## Key Findings

Gemini-2.5 Pro achieved highest accuracy of 90.45% on ArtDL, outperforming ResNet50 baselines. Performance varied significantly by dataset, with Wikidata showing lower accuracies (best: 64.86% by SigLIP). Enriching prompts with Iconclass descriptions generally improved zero-shot performance, while few-shot learning produced mixed results with mostly lower accuracy.
