## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Multi-modal evaluation combining artwork images of Christian saints with natural language prompts. Three test configurations: (1) classification with class labels only, (2) classification with Iconclass descriptions, and (3) few-shot learning with exemplar images

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** single-label image classification, zero-shot classification, few-shot learning, iconographic recognition

**Detailed Description:** Single-label classification of Christian saints in religious paintings, requiring recognition of complex iconographic symbols and religious themes. Tasks involve zero-shot classification using semantic labels or detailed iconographic descriptions, and few-shot learning with visual exemplars

## Summary

This paper introduces a benchmark for evaluating vision-language models and multimodal LLMs on Christian iconography classification using three datasets (ArtDL, ICONCLASS, Wikidata) with Iconclass codes. The study compares zero-shot and few-shot performance across different prompt configurations, finding that multimodal LLMs (Gemini-2.5 Pro, GPT-4o) generally outperform traditional supervised baselines.

## Key Findings

Gemini-2.5 Pro achieved highest performance with 90.45% accuracy on ArtDL, while GPT-4o reached 88.20%. Performance dropped significantly on Wikidata dataset where SigLIP-SO400M achieved best results at 64.86%. Enriching prompts with iconographic descriptions generally improved zero-shot performance, but few-shot learning produced mixed results with mostly lower accuracy scores.
