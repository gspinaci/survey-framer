## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Text-only bilingual processing of bibliographic metadata (titles and abstracts) in English and German mapped to the German Integrated Authority File (GND) subject taxonomy.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** extreme multi-label text classification, semantic retrieval, subject tagging

**Detailed Description:** Predicting and ranking a target set of top-k semantically relevant subject terms from the massive GND taxonomy (up to 204,739 terms) for various electronic library records (articles, books, theses, reports).

## Summary

This paper outlines SemEval-2025 Task 5: LLMs4Subjects, a shared task evaluating automated subject tagging for technical library catalog records in English and German using the GND taxonomy. It evaluates various machine learning and LLM-based systems over both quantitative benchmarks and manual qualitative validations performed by expert librarians.

## Key Findings

Traditional text classifiers (like Annif) and fast retrieval models (like RUC Team) achieved the highest quantitative recall scores (up to 0.66). However, in manual qualitative evaluations by professional librarians, the DNB-AI-Project—which used a zero/few-shot ensemble of LLMs (such as Llama-3.1-70B)—secured the highest precision, demonstrating the strong real-world taxonomy-mapping capabilities of LLMs without deep task-specific finetuning.
