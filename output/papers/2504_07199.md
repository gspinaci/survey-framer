## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Bilingual (English and German) textual library catalog metadata consisting of document titles and abstracts.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** extreme multi-label classification, subject indexing, taxonomy mapping, ranking

**Detailed Description:** Predicting and ranking a customized top-k list of relevant GND subject headings based on bibliographic text (titles and abstracts), testing extreme multi-label semantic mapping to a controlled vocabulary.

## Summary

This paper introduces SemEval-2025 Task 5: LLMs4Subjects, a shared task evaluating how well LLMs can automate subject tagging for library records in English and German using the GND taxonomy. It benchmarks multiple approaches, ranging from dense retrieval and traditional extreme multi-label classification to generative LLM ensembles with few-shot prompting, assessing them via both quantitative metrics and professional qualitative evaluations.

## Key Findings

In quantitative evaluations, traditional text classifiers and embedding-based retrieval models achieved the highest overall recall. However, in qualitative evaluations by library specialists, a zero-shot/few-shot LLM ensemble with no fine-tuning (DNB-AI-Project) achieved the highest precision and recall, demonstrating the strong generalization capabilities of LLMs over heavily fine-tuned systems.
