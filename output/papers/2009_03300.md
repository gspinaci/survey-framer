**Task Types:** multiple-choice question answering, factual recognition, logical deduction, moral reasoning, legal rule application

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates declarative knowledge retrieval in history and religion, logical deduction in formal logic, moral reasoning in everyday scenarios, and legal rule application in professional law cases.

## Summary

This paper introduces the Massive Multitask Language Understanding (MMLU) benchmark, a test spanning 57 subjects designed to measure LLM multitask knowledge and reasoning. By evaluating models in zero-shot and few-shot transfer settings across 15,908 multiple-choice questions, it aims to test the breadth and depth of real-world knowledge acquired during pretraining.

## Key Findings

The 175B parameter GPT-3 model achieved 43.9% average accuracy, scoring well above the 25% random baseline but far below the 89.8% human expert-level estimate. The evaluation revealed that LLM capabilities were highly lopsided, showing particular weakness in calculation-heavy STEM tasks and socially sensitive subjects like morality and law, and exhibited poor calibration with confidence scores diverging from actual accuracy by up to 24%.
