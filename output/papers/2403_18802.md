## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Evaluates text-only outputs generated by language models in response to open-ended, fact-seeking text prompts.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** long-form generation, factual recall, factual precision verification, factual recognition

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates generative LLMs on long-form, open-domain generation by breaking down responses into 'atomic facts' and verifying their accuracy (factual precision) and coverage (factual recall) against search results. It tests literal factual recall rather than deeper historical causal analysis or interpretive argumentation.

## Summary

The paper introduces LongFact, a multi-topic dataset containing 2,280 prompts across 38 categories designed to evaluate the factuality of long-form LLM responses. To scale evaluation, it presents SAFE, an automated agentic pipeline that deconstructs model responses into atomic facts and verifies them via Google Search, alongside F1@K, a novel metric combining factual precision and length-bounded recall.

## Key Findings

Larger language models generally exhibit superior long-form factuality. The automated SAFE rater achieves 72% agreement with crowdsourced human annotators, outperforms them on disagreement cases (76% accuracy to 19%), and is over 20 times cheaper.
