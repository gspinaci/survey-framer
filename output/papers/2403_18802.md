## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** A unimodal text-based benchmark consisting of fact-seeking prompts designed to elicit multi-paragraph, long-form text responses.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** long-form open-domain question answering, information extraction, atomic fact verification

**Detailed Description:** Decomposes long-form generative responses into independent, self-contained atomic facts, filters them for prompt relevance, and verifies each claim against Google Search using an iterative, multi-step LLM-agent reasoning process.

## Summary

This paper presents LongFact, a new prompt set spanning 38 topics designed to test the factuality of large language models in generating long-form answers. To automate evaluation, the authors introduce SAFE (Search-Augmented Factuality Evaluator), which uses an LLM to split answers into atomic facts and verify them using Google Search, as well as an F1@K metric to balance factual precision with a user-preferred response length.

## Key Findings

The automated evaluator SAFE matches or beats human crowd-workers, agreeing with human annotations 72% of the time and winning 76% of disagreement cases, while being more than 20 times cheaper ($0.19 vs $4.00 per response). Benchmarking 13 models reveals that larger models (such as GPT-4-Turbo, Gemini-Ultra, and PaLM-2-L-IT-RLHF) perform best, showing that scaling primarily improves factual recall.
