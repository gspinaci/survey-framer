## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Combines text and images in a multiple-choice format. It features a novel 'vision-only' setting where textual questions and options are embedded directly into images (as screenshots or photos), requiring models to visually process and holistically integrate both text and figures simultaneously.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** multiple-choice QA, visual reasoning, text extraction, cross-modal thematic interpretation, stylistic and aesthetic category recognition

**Detailed Description:** Involves factual and stylistic recognition of artworks, identifying architectural systems, music theory analysis from notation, and historical interpretation of political cartoons. These complex visual-textual spatial reasoning and interpretive tasks are ultimately flattened into a 10-option multiple-choice evaluation format.

## Summary

MMMU-Pro introduces a more robust evaluation framework for the Massive Multi-discipline Multimodal Understanding (MMMU) benchmark to prevent shortcut exploitation by MLLMs. It eliminates text-only solvable questions, scales the candidate options from 4 to 10 choices, and introduces a vision-only setting where questions are embedded directly into images. This design pushes models to truly integrate visual and textual modalities, exposing significant performance gaps in current frontier models.

## Key Findings

All evaluated MLLMs showed significant performance drops on MMMU-Pro compared to MMMU, with accuracy declines ranging from 16.8% to 26.9%. GPT-4o achieved the highest overall score with 54.0% in the standard 10-option setting and 49.7% in the vision-only setting. Chain of Thought (CoT) prompting showed strong gains in structured reasoning fields but was found to be ineffective or even detrimental in subjective domains like Art and Design.
