## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Consists of text-only questions and multimodal questions (around 14%) where a text question is accompanied by an image reference.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** multiple-choice question answering, exact-match short-answer question answering, translation of ancient scripts, phonological and syllabic analysis, mathematical reasoning, scientific chemical reasoning

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates high-level academic tasks including translating ancient Palmyrene script from tombstone images, classifying closed and open syllables in Tiberian Biblical Hebrew pronunciation, reasoning over natural cotransformations in category theory, and identifying thermal pericyclic cascade steps in organic chemistry.

## Summary

This paper introduces Humanity's Last Exam (HLE), a multi-modal benchmark consisting of 2,500 highly challenging questions designed to test models at the limit of academic human knowledge. Developed by global subject-matter experts, HLE features a mixture of multiple-choice and exact-match questions that currently stump frontier LLMs, exposing substantial gaps in their expert-level reasoning capabilities.

## Key Findings

State-of-the-art models demonstrate low performance on HLE, with the highest-performing text-only reasoning model, o3-mini (high), achieving just 13.4% accuracy. Additionally, models show poor calibration, with RMS calibration errors exceeding 70% across all evaluated systems, highlighting a strong tendency to confidently output incorrect answers.
