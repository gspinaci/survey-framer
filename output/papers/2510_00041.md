## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Pairs comic and manga pages (image) with natural language multiple-choice questions, open-ended structured queries, and dialog translation tasks (text).

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** factual recognition, visual reasoning, logical reasoning, cultural conflict detection, machine translation

**Detailed Description:** Progresses from factual visual identification of background cultures and representative objects, to visual and logical reasoning for detecting cultural co-occurrence conflicts, and finally machine translation of comic dialogue across diverse target languages.

## Summary

This paper introduces the Comics Cross-Cultural Benchmark (C3B), a specialized multimodal evaluation suite containing 2,220 comic images and 18,789 QA pairs. The benchmark leverages comics and manga to evaluate MLLMs on progressive cultural awareness tasks ranging from basic object extraction to cultural conflict reasoning and cross-lingual dialogue translation. Systematic evaluation of 11 open-source MLLMs reveals a substantial performance gap between AI and human baselines, especially in understanding cultural conflicts and low-resource languages.

## Key Findings

The top-performing model, Qwen2.5-VL, leads across most tasks but still lags significantly behind human performance, particularly in high-level cultural conflict detection. MLLMs consistently perform well on dominant cultures (e.g., Japan, China) and high-resource languages (English), but struggle severely with lesser-known cultures (e.g., Finnish, Somali) and low-resource translations, highlighting a persistent Western-centric and majoritarian cultural bias.
