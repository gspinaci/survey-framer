## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Text-only benchmark using narrative passages from novels where models must predict the final word of a target sentence given broader discourse context

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** word prediction, discourse comprehension, coreference resolution

**Detailed Description:** Word prediction requiring broad discourse understanding where the target word cannot be guessed from local sentence context alone but becomes predictable with full passage context. Tasks involve coreference resolution, bridging inferences, and understanding narrative coherence.

## Summary

LAMBADA introduces a word prediction benchmark using narrative passages where humans can guess the final word with full context but not from the last sentence alone. The dataset consists of 10,022 passages from novels designed to test computational models' ability to understand broad discourse context rather than relying on local linguistic cues.

## Key Findings

All tested language models (RNN, LSTM, N-gram, Memory Networks) achieved near 0% accuracy on LAMBADA while performing well (15-22% accuracy) on a control dataset, demonstrating that current models fail at discourse-level understanding despite strong local language modeling capabilities.
