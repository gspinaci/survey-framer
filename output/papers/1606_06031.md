## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Monolingual English narrative passages consisting of a context (average 4.6 sentences) and a single target sentence with the final word masked.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** word prediction, cloze task, co-reference resolution, discourse coherence analysis, pragmatic inference

**Detailed Description:** Predicting the final word of a narrative passage, requiring the model to maintain discourse-level coherence over a broad textual window, track character referents, and resolve pragmatic contexts.

## Summary

The paper introduces the LAMBADA dataset, a word prediction benchmark consisting of 10,022 narrative passages designed to test a model's ability to utilize broad discourse context. Passages are selected such that human evaluators can easily guess the final word when given the full multi-sentence context, but cannot do so when exposed only to the final target sentence.

## Key Findings

All evaluated neural and statistical language models (including LSTM, RNN, and Memory Networks) failed to achieve even 1% accuracy on the LAMBADA dataset, despite showing strong performance (up to 21.9% accuracy) on an unfiltered control dataset. A simple baseline of picking a random capitalized word from the passage achieved 7.3% accuracy, significantly outperforming all tested language models.
