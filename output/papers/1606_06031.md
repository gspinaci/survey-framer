## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Text-only benchmark consisting of narrative passages with context sentences and a target sentence where the last word must be predicted

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** word prediction, discourse understanding, co-reference resolution

**Detailed Description:** Tasks require predicting words that can only be guessed with broad discourse context, involving co-reference resolution, inference of prototypical participants in events, and understanding narrative continuations

## Summary

LAMBADA introduces a word prediction benchmark requiring broad discourse context understanding, where target words can be guessed by humans when given full passages but not from local sentence context alone. The dataset consists of 10,022 narrative passages from novels, filtered through human annotation to ensure broad context dependency.

## Key Findings

All tested language models performed poorly on LAMBADA (≤1% accuracy) compared to 19-21% on control data, with the best model being N-Gram with cache. 80% of LAMBADA items have target words appearing in context, with proper nouns comprising 48% of targets.
