## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Text-only benchmark consisting of narrative passages from novels where models must predict the final word of a target sentence given broader discourse context

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** word prediction, discourse understanding, coreference resolution

**Detailed Description:** Word prediction requiring broad discourse context understanding including coreference resolution, bridging inferences, and reasoning about prototypical participants in events described in narrative passages

## Summary

This paper introduces LAMBADA, a word prediction benchmark that requires models to use broad discourse context rather than local sentence context to predict the final word of narrative passages. The benchmark consists of 10,022 passages from novels where humans can guess the target word given the full context but not from the final sentence alone.

## Key Findings

All tested language models (RNN, LSTM, Memory Networks, N-gram models) performed poorly on LAMBADA with accuracy below 1%, while achieving 15-22% accuracy on a control dataset, demonstrating the challenge of broad context understanding for existing models.
