## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** Text

**Description:** Passages of text consisting of a narrative context (average of 4.6 sentences) followed by a target sentence with a missing final word.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** Word prediction, Discourse tracking, Co-reference resolution, Pragmatic inference

**Detailed Description:** Predicting a target word that is contextually constrained by broader discourse and narrative structures, requiring reasoning about character interactions, event sequences, and semantic associations across multiple sentences.

## Summary

The paper introduces the LAMBADA dataset, a text-based benchmark consisting of narrative passages designed to test language models on broad context comprehension. The target words are selected to be easily guessable by humans when presented with the full context, but impossible to guess from the final sentence alone. Baseline results show that contemporary language models struggle significantly, failing to achieve even 1% accuracy.

## Key Findings

None of the tested models (RNN, LSTM, Memory Network, and N-gram variants) exceeded 1% accuracy on the LAMBADA dataset, despite achieving up to 21.9% accuracy on an unfiltered control set of similar shape, highlighting a severe limitation in long-range discourse comprehension.
