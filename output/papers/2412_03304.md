## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** A mono-modal text benchmark featuring multiple-choice questions from MMLU translated or post-edited across 42 languages.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** multiple-choice question answering, factual recognition, cultural reasoning, geographical reasoning, dialect awareness evaluation

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates models on multi-subject factual knowledge and contextual reasoning. By categorizing questions into Culturally-Sensitive (requiring localized historical, geographic, or cultural frames) and Culturally-Agnostic subsets, it assesses whether models can reason outside of Western-centric epistemic frameworks.

## Summary

This paper presents Global-MMLU, a high-quality multilingual adaptation of the MMLU dataset spanning 42 languages. The authors introduce annotations that partition the dataset into culturally-sensitive and culturally-agnostic subsets, exposing a heavy Western-centric bias in the original English MMLU. By evaluating multiple generative models on these subsets, the paper provides a more nuanced framework for assessing multilingual performance and multicultural alignment.

## Key Findings

The authors find that 28% of MMLU questions require culturally sensitive knowledge, with 86.5% of those being Western-specific and 84.9% of geographic questions focusing on North America or Europe. Evaluating 14 state-of-the-art LLMs reveals substantial volatility in model rankings between the culturally-agnostic and culturally-sensitive subsets (averaging 5.7 rank changes and 7.3 position shifts on CS subsets), a phenomenon that is particularly pronounced for smaller models and lower-resource languages.
