## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Textual multiple-choice question answering across 42 languages, combining human translations, post-edits, and machine-translated content.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** multiple-choice question answering, cultural sensitivity classification, cross-lingual knowledge retrieval

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates factual knowledge and reasoning across multiple disciplines, specifically dividing tasks into culturally-sensitive (requiring localized cultural, geographic, or dialectal context) and culturally-agnostic subsets.

## Summary

Global-MMLU (GMMLU) is a multilingual evaluation benchmark across 42 languages designed to analyze and address linguistic and cultural biases in machine-translated versions of the MMLU dataset. The authors systematically annotate MMLU to identify culturally-sensitive and culturally-agnostic subsets, exposing a heavy Western-centric bias in the original questions, and release a high-quality human-verified translation to enable more accurate global evaluations.

## Key Findings

The analysis reveals that 28% of MMLU questions require culturally-sensitive (CS) knowledge, with 86.5% of those being Western-centric and 84.9% of geographic questions focusing on North America or Europe. Evaluating models on CS vs. culturally-agnostic (CA) subsets causes significant ranking volatility (averaging 5.7 rank changes on CS vs. 3.4 on CA), showing that standard translated MMLU distorts true multilingual capabilities.
