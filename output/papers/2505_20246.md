## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image, audio, video

**Description:** A multimodal setup combining textual questions with visual sources (manuscripts, maps, illustrations, inscriptions), audio recordings (oral history), and historical video clips.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** Bibliographic Retrieval, Source Identification, Source Processing, Historical Analysis, Interdisciplinary Integration, Cultural Contextualization

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates complex historical inquiry skills, including factual retrieval from primary sources, processing non-textual or damaged formats, causal reasoning, interdisciplinary synthesis (e.g., archaeology and linguistics), and decoding cultural contexts.

## Summary

This paper introduces HistBench, a multimodal, multilingual benchmark of 414 expert-curated questions designed to assess historical reasoning. To overcome the limitations of generalist LLMs on these tasks, the authors propose HistAgent, a domain-specialized framework equipped with tools for historical OCR, translation, and academic literature search.

## Key Findings

State-of-the-art models equipped with generic web search struggle on HistBench (e.g., GPT-4o achieves 18.60% pass@1). The custom HistAgent framework, built on GPT-4o, significantly outperforms these baselines, reaching 27.54% pass@1 and 36.47% pass@2, while maintaining strong performance on general benchmarks like GAIA.
