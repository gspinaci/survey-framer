## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Text-only multi-choice questions in Chinese, evaluated using zero-shot/few-shot answer-only and chain-of-thought prompting.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** multiple-choice question answering, factual knowledge retrieval, multi-step reasoning

**Detailed Description:** Evaluates factual knowledge and reasoning capabilities through multiple-choice questions across four difficulty tiers (middle school, high school, college, and professional).

## Summary

C-Eval is a comprehensive, multi-level Chinese evaluation suite designed to assess the advanced knowledge and reasoning abilities of foundation models. It contains 13,948 multiple-choice questions spanning 52 disciplines across middle school, high school, college, and professional levels. It also includes C-Eval Hard, a specialized subset of demanding math and science subjects designed to test advanced reasoning.

## Key Findings

GPT-4 was the only model to exceed a 60% average accuracy, scoring 66.4% in zero-shot and 68.7% in five-shot settings. Chinese-oriented models, such as GLM-130B, narrowed the performance gap with ChatGPT on humanities and social sciences but significantly lagged on STEM subjects and the advanced reasoning tasks in C-Eval Hard.
