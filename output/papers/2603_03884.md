## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text

**Description:** Monolingual textual data consisting of segments from OCR-processed historical Czech documents.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** topic presence detection, word-level topic localization

**Detailed Description:** Evaluating the detection of topic presence at the document level and the precise word-level identification of overlapping or non-contiguous text spans that express a predefined topic's name and description.

## Summary

This paper presents CzechTopic, a novel human-annotated benchmark for zero-shot topic localization using historical Czech archives. It introduces a word-level span localization task evaluated against human agreement and benchmarks several state-of-the-art LLMs as well as BERT-based cross-encoders fine-tuned on a distilled dataset.

## Key Findings

The best-performing LLM (GPT-5-2) achieved a word-level F1 of 61.1 and IoU of 48.7, remaining significantly below the human baseline (68.7 F1, 57.2 IoU). Fine-tuned BERT cross-encoders (e.g., robeczech at 48.3 F1) performed competitively, outperforming several larger generative LLMs.
