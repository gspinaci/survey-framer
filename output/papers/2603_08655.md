## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, tables, images

**Description:** Multi-modal reasoning combining unstructured text, highly complex tabular data, and visual charts or figures extracted from PDF documents.

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** document retrieval, tabular data extraction, longitudinal financial reconciliation, statistical analysis, mathematical calculation, visual reasoning

**Detailed Description:** Tasks require locating information across a large 100-year corpus, parsing nested tables, adjusting figures for inflation using external CPI data, fitting ordinary least squares (OLS) linear regressions, and reasoning over trend lines in charts.

## Summary

OfficeQA Pro is an enterprise benchmark containing 133 questions designed to evaluate AI agents on grounded reasoning over a 100-year corpus of U.S. Treasury Bulletins. It requires systems to perform multi-step document retrieval, extract data from complex hierarchical tables, and execute advanced quantitative and statistical computations. The benchmark demonstrates that state-of-the-art agents struggle on these tasks, and highlights high-quality document parsing as a critical bottleneck for success.

## Key Findings

Frontier models score under 5% accuracy on parametric knowledge alone. The best-performing agent (Claude Opus 4.6) achieves 48.1% accuracy on the full PDF corpus, which improves to 54.1% when utilizing Databricks' ai_parse_document for text parsing, and reaches a peak of 66.9% when provided with oracle parsed pages.
