## Benchmark Modalities

**Modality Types:** text, image

**Description:** Evaluates agents on text (both unstructured prose and structured tabular data) and images (visual interpretation of historical figures, charts, and scanned PDF pages).

## Analytical Tasks

**Task Types:** document retrieval, tabular data parsing, quantitative reasoning, statistical analysis, temporal data reconciliation, visual reasoning

**Detailed Description:** Requires multi-step reasoning across unstructured text and complex nested tables, including longitudinal financial analysis (e.g., ordinary least squares linear regression), inflation adjustments, temporal revision verification, and high-precision arithmetic computations.

## Summary

OfficeQA Pro is an enterprise benchmark designed to evaluate AI agents on end-to-end grounded reasoning over a heterogeneous 100-year archive of U.S. Treasury Bulletins. It contains 133 highly complex and verifiable questions requiring precise document parsing, multi-document retrieval, and quantitative mathematical modeling to measure SOTA model limitations.

## Key Findings

Frontier agents struggle on over half of the questions, with the strongest agent (Claude Opus 4.6) scoring 48.1% when processing raw PDFs. Converting the PDFs to a structured representation yields a 16.1% relative accuracy gain on average and significantly decreases latency and cost. Surprisingly, AI agents consistently outperform human annotators in both speed and accuracy on a subset evaluation.
