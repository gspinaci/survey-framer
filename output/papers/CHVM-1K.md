**Detailed Description:** Evaluating LLM outputs against international cultural heritage ethics, measuring representation biases, and evaluating factual consistency and the preservation of cultural narratives.

## Summary

This paper presents CHVM-1K, a benchmark designed to investigate value misalignment in texts generated by LLMs regarding global cultural heritage. By analyzing model responses through the lens of heritage preservation ethics and representational bias, it provides a structured framework to quantify deviations from established historical accuracy and values.

## Key Findings

The benchmark provides a framework and dataset of 1,000 cases to audit LLMs, highlighting potential risks of misaligned cultural narratives, Western-centric bias, and factual inaccuracies in heritage contexts.
